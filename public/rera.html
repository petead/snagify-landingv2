What is RERA

The authority behind every Dubai rental

RERA — the Real Estate Regulatory Agency — is the branch of Dubai Land Department that governs all landlord-tenant relationships in the emirate. It sets the rules, and its Rental Dispute Center (RDC) settles conflicts.

When a deposit dispute reaches the RDC, the first thing they ask for is a signed, dated inspection report from both move-in and move-out. Without it, the case is decided on testimony alone — which rarely goes in the claimant's favor.

Common questions

On RERA, the RDC, and how Snagify fits into Dubai's rental framework.

Is a Snagify report officially accepted by the RDC?
Yes. The RDC accepts any inspection report that meets the documented-evidence standard: dual signatures, timestamp, photo evidence, and property identification. Snagify reports are structured to meet all of these criteria. They have been accepted in dispute proceedings in Dubai.
What if only one party signs the report?
A single-signature report still holds evidential value, particularly for the party that did sign. Snagify documents refusals: if one party declines to sign, this refusal is recorded in the report with a timestamp. The RDC factors refusal-to-sign into their assessment.
Does it matter which format the report is in?
Yes. Handwritten forms, WhatsApp messages, or editable Word documents can all be challenged as altered or incomplete. A tamper-proof, digitally-signed PDF with a verifiable hash is significantly harder to dispute. Snagify generates this format by default.
Do I need to use Snagify for the check-in AND check-out?
Ideally yes. The RDC comparison looks at the condition at move-in versus move-out. Having both inspections in the same system makes the comparison irrefutable. That said, even a Snagify check-out report submitted alongside a prior paper check-in adds significant weight to a claim.
Is the check-in really free?
Yes, always. Check-in inspections are permanently free for individual landlords and tenants. Credits are only consumed for check-out inspections. Professional plans include a monthly credit allocation for both check-ins and check-outs.
How long should I keep my inspection reports?
The RDC statute of limitations for tenancy disputes is generally 3 years from the date of the alleged breach. We recommend keeping inspection records for at least 3 years. All Snagify reports are stored indefinitely on your account and can be re-downloaded at any time.
